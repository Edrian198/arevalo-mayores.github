Luzon Datum

Introduction

Luzon Datum of 1911/Station Balanacan. This Philippine Marker is located in Barangay Hinanggayon, within the town of Mogpog.According to marinduque.gov.ph, its institutionalization as geodetic datum origin is traced to the surveying and mapping activities in the Philippines of the then United States Coast and Geodetic Survey (USCGS). With the new territorial claim of the US following the Spanish-American War, the responsibilities of the USCGS grew which included the conduct of various scientific and cartographic initiatives in the Philippine Islands beginning 1901.

The Luzon Datum is a geodetic reference system used in the Philippines to establish accurate coordinates for mapping and surveying purposes. While it provides a standardized framework for spatial data across the country, its specific implementation in Marinduque would involve surveying and geodetic measurements conducted on the island.Geodetic surveying in Marinduque, as in other parts of the Philippines, would involve determining precise coordinates for various landmarks, boundaries, and geographic features using the Luzon Datum as a reference. This data is crucial for activities such as land use planning, infrastructure development, and navigation.
